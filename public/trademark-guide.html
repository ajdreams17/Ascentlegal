    What's Inside the Guide

    Seven Mistakes. Real Consequences.

    Each one is avoidable — if you know what to look for. Here's a preview of what the guide covers.

    01

    Skipping the Trademark Search

    A Google search is not a trademark search. Discover what a real clearance search looks like — and why skipping it is the most expensive mistake you can make.

    Most Common
    02

    Choosing the Wrong Classes

    File in the wrong category and your registration protects nothing in your actual market — even after the USPTO approves it.

    Costly
    03

    Waiting Too Long to File

    Every day you delay is an open window for a competitor to file first — and legally lock you out of your own name.

    Time-Sensitive
    04

    Using Merely Descriptive Terms

    Your brand name itself can get your application rejected. Learn the spectrum of trademark strength — and where your name falls.

    Hidden Trap
    05

    Ignoring International Rights

    A U.S. registration doesn't protect you abroad. Trademark squatters actively monitor USPTO filings to register your mark in key markets first.

    Global Risk
    06

    Inconsistent Trademark Use

    How you use your mark after registration is just as important as filing. Misuse — or no use — can invalidate your rights entirely.

    Post-Filing
    07

    Failing to Monitor & Enforce

    A trademark you don't defend is a trademark you can lose. Learn the practical monitoring approach that protects your brand long after registration.
